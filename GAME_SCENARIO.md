# TI2MI — Unit Conversion Evolution Game

## 🎯 Overview

**TI2MI** is a browser-based educational game that teaches school-age children (ages 7–14) to convert between units of measurement. Players solve increasingly difficult conversion problems to advance through **15 evolution stages**. Each playthrough uses a different **evolution theme**, giving the game massive replay value.

---

## 📐 Unit Categories

| Category | Units |
|----------|-------|
| **Time** | seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, months, years |
| **Volume** | ml, cl, dl, l |
| **Length** | mm, cm, dm, m, km |
| **Weight** | g, kg, ton |
| **Area** | cm², m², km² *(later stages)* |

---

## 🧩 Difficulty Progression (15 Levels)

*Problem style inspired by Czech elementary school unit conversion exercises (e.g., M5103 curriculum).*
*Problems are generated dynamically with randomized values — examples below show the pattern/style only.*

### Tier 1 — Foundation (Levels 1–3)
- **Level 1:** Direct 1-step conversions, round numbers
  - *"How many minutes is 120 seconds?"*
  - *"Convert 3 km to meters."*
  - *"How many ml are in 2 liters?"*
- **Level 2:** Direct 1-step conversions, non-round numbers
  - *"Express 450 cm in meters."*
  - *"How many seconds is 3.5 minutes?"*
  - *"Convert 1750 ml to liters."*
- **Level 3:** Reverse & fill-in-the-gap conversions
  - *"___ mm + 600 mm = 1 m"*
  - *"5 dl + ___ ml = 1 l"*
  - *"___ seconds + 40 s = 2 min"*

### Tier 2 — Growing (Levels 4–6)
- **Level 4:** Fractions and halves
  - *"What is half a kilogram in grams?"*
  - *"How many dl is half a liter?"*
  - *"Express ½ hour in seconds."*
- **Level 5:** Quarters, thirds, and simple fraction-unit combos
  - *"What is a quarter of a meter in cm?"*
  - *"How many minutes is ⅓ of an hour?"*
  - *"Express ¾ of a liter in ml."*
- **Level 6:** Multi-step same-category chains
  - *"Convert 7200 seconds to hours."*
  - *"How many cm are in 0.04 km?"*
  - *"Express 15 000 ml in liters and dl."*

### Tier 3 — Intermediate (Levels 7–9)
- **Level 7:** Adding/subtracting mixed units
  - *"3 m 40 cm + 260 cm = ___ m"*
  - *"2 h 15 min − 90 min = ___ min"*
  - *"1 l 200 ml − 8 dl = ___ ml"*
- **Level 8:** Simple word problems with division/sharing
  - *"A ribbon is 4.5 m long. You cut it into 6 equal pieces. How many cm is each piece?"*
  - *"A jug holds 2.4 liters. You pour it equally into 8 cups. How many ml per cup?"*
- **Level 9:** Multiplication in context, repeated quantities
  - *"A clock chimes every 15 minutes. How many chimes in 3 hours?"*
  - *"Each tile is 30 cm wide. How many tiles fit along a 3.6 m wall?"*

### Tier 4 — Advanced (Levels 10–12)
- **Level 10:** Time arithmetic, schedules, duration calculations
  - *"A movie starts at 19:45 and is 1 h 55 min long. When does it end?"*
  - *"A bus left at 07:20 and arrived at 09:05. How long was the trip?"*
- **Level 11:** Proportional / fraction-of-whole problems
  - *"A runner finishes a race in 48 min. Another is 25% faster. What's the faster time?"*
  - *"A rope is 12 m. After cutting off ⅓, you divide the rest into 4 equal parts. How many cm is each?"*
- **Level 12:** Multi-step movement & rate word problems
  - *"Two friends walk toward each other from 1 km apart. One walks 70 cm per step, the other 55 cm. They take the same number of steps. How far did each walk?"*
  - *"Anna walks 4 km/h and leaves at 8:00. Ben walks 5 km/h and leaves at 8:15 from the same point. When does Ben catch Anna?"*

### Tier 5 — Expert (Levels 13–15)
- **Level 13:** Combined category problems (two unit types)
  - *"A snail crawls 15 cm per minute. How far in meters does it get in 2 hours?"*
  - *"A tap fills 250 ml every 10 seconds. How many liters per hour?"*
- **Level 14:** Complex real-world scenarios
  - *"A recipe for 4 servings needs 600 ml of milk and 1.2 kg of flour. What do you need for 7 servings?"*
  - *"A car uses 7 l per 100 km. Fuel costs 1.40 €/L. How much does a 250 km trip cost?"*
- **Level 15:** Multi-step chained problems (3+ steps)
  - *"A pool is 10 m × 5 m × 1.2 m. A hose fills 3 l/sec. How many hours to fill it?"*
  - *"A factory packs 500 ml bottles into crates of 12. How many crates to ship 360 liters?"*
  - *"You drive at 80 km/h for 2 h 15 min, then 60 km/h for 45 min. Total distance in meters?"*

---

## 🎮 Game Mechanics

- Each level presents **5 problems** to solve
- Player must solve **4 out of 5 correctly** to advance
- Wrong answers show a brief explanation and the correct solution
- A **streak bonus** (3+ correct in a row) triggers a fun animation
- After level 15, player receives a **completion badge** for that theme
- Timer is optional (can be enabled for extra challenge)

## 🌐 Localization (3 Languages)

The game supports **English**, **Czech (Čeština)**, and **Russian (Русский)**.

### What Gets Translated
| Element | Example (EN → CZ → RU) |
|---------|------------------------|
| UI labels | "Level 5" → "Úroveň 5" → "Уровень 5" |
| Problem text | "How many ml in 2 liters?" → "Kolik ml je ve 2 litrech?" → "Сколько мл в 2 литрах?" |
| Unit names | "minutes" → "minuty" → "минуты" |
| Word problems | Full narrative localization — culturally adapted, not machine-translated |
| Evolution stage names | "Cave Human" → "Jeskynní člověk" → "Пещерный человек" |
| Feedback messages | "Correct! 🎉" → "Správně! 🎉" → "Правильно! 🎉" |
| Menu / settings | All UI chrome |

### Localization Notes
- **Unit symbols** (m, km, l, ml, s, min, h) are international and do NOT change
- **Number formatting:** EN uses "." for decimals; CZ and RU use "," (e.g., 3.5 vs 3,5) — the game must accept both formats as valid input regardless of language
- **Currency in word problems:** EN uses €/$ , CZ uses Kč , RU uses ₽ — adapted per locale
- **Cultural context in word problems:** Names and scenarios adjusted (e.g., EN: "Anna", CZ: "Anička", RU: "Аня")
- Language is selectable at any time from settings; does not affect progress

### Implementation Approach
- All text stored in **i18n JSON files** per language: `en.json`, `cs.json`, `ru.json`
- Problem templates use **placeholders** for values: `"How many {targetUnit} in {value} {sourceUnit}?"`
- Word problems are **hand-written per language** (not auto-translated) to feel natural
- Adding a new language = adding one JSON file + word problem translations

## 🔧 Technical Notes

- **Platform:** Web browser (responsive — works on tablets and desktops)
- **Stack:** Vite + React + TypeScript
- **Art Style:** Emoji-based evolution stages with themed color accents
- **Sound:** Cheerful background music per theme + SFX for correct/wrong/level-up *(planned)*
- **Accessibility:** High contrast via color schemes, keyboard navigation, accepts both "." and "," for decimals
- **Progress Saving:** Browser localStorage with multi-profile support — designed for easy future migration to cloud
- **Localization:** English, Czech, Russian — template-based with i18n JSON files (react-i18next)
- **Color Schemes:** 4 themes (Sunset, Ocean, Forest, Candy) — selectable per profile
- **No backend in Phase 1** — everything runs client-side
